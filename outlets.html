So much music!

There's so many different options for people who want to get into music. From becoming a total band nerd to starting a garage band, I believe there's something for everyone. Now I don't know everything, but I do love music, and I can list a handful of ways for people to incorporate music into their lives:

  • Join your school's concert band
  • Join your school's marching band
  • Join your school's choir
  • Join a church choir
  • Join an acapella group
  • Audition for a musical
  • Start a band with your friends
  • Take private lessons(for singing or an instrument you play)
  • Watch online tutorials on how to play an instrument you're interested in
  • Take a songwriting/composing class

Of course there are ENDLESS options, but these (in my opinion) are the main ones. So, let's jump right in and talk about them!

Band

(I know I've already talked about band a lot, so we're gonna get that out of the way first). There are a lot of great things that band can offer, like the opportunity to make 50+ new friends that you will play with for the next several years. Band is one of the best ways (in my opinion) to dive into the technicalities of music. From day one, students are taught how to read sheet music, how to play their instrument, and how to connect the two. It allows you to connect to music through style and interpretation. It is a lot of hard work and practice, and everyone has to do their part for the band to sound great. Joining band opens up so many new opportunities for aspiring musicians, like playing at special events(openings of new buildings, weddings, other ceremonious events), ajudicated performances, or specific honor bands. There's just so many great things about it.

Marching band is similar to regular band in some aspects: you get music, practice, and perform; but it's also much more than that. The main difference in that it involves physical activity, or at least a lot more of it than regular band. Everyone still has to do their part in order for the band to function properly, even more so in this case because there's a visual aspect to it. Marching band is essentially concert band (aka regular band) on steroids. Every year, a typical marching band performs a new show. Everyone in the band gets a really long piece of music, a number, and a set of spots on the field (drill cards). Once everyone learns their music, they have to put the music together with their drill spots and move from one to the other using the music as a guide. Usually a band would start learning it's show during band camp over the summer, and they have about 4 months to learn and perfect it for their state contests. Marching band will take over those 4 months out of the year as long as you do it, but it is 100% worth it. Joining marching band is hands down the best decision I've ever made. Joining either concert or marching band (or both), is something that I would definitely recommemd to everyone. Everyone goes through their ups and downs together in band, and they become a family. What more could you want than a big family of band nerds?

Singing

Now, I'm not an expert on choir, and I can't speak from experience, but I have many friends that are in choir. Choir is definitely a good outlet for people who don't want to play an instrument but still want to be involved in music. Just like band, everyone is expected to do their part in order for the ensemble to sound great. Singing is quite possibly the best way to interpret music, because it uses style, but it also has lyrics, whereas band doesn't. It requires a similar work ethic to concert band, and in some ways it can be much harder. The voice is an ever changing instrument, and can be unpredictable at times, and while that makes it hard to control, it also makes it one of the most interesting to develop. There are many different types of choirs that people can join, between school choirs and church choirs, there's a good range of them that people could consider. In other words, if you love singing, join choir.

There are other singing related things besides choir that people could consider, such as acapella groups or musicals. On top of having multiple choirs, my school also has an acapella group and a spring musical every year. I would basically describe an acapella group as a self-run, more free flowing mini choir (if that makes any sense). And in an acapella group, everyone has a conpletely different role to fill. Which leads me to my next topic: musicals. Auditioning for a musical is probably the most outgoing way to get into music. It requires a lot of confidence and maturity, and a musical can either turn out really good or really bad depending on those qualities. Again, I can't speak from experience on these topics, but that's my interpretation of them. I would highly encourage the bolder vocalists out there to consider these options as well. It would be really fun!

Taking Lessons

This subject is one that I can speak about from experience. Whether you're taking private lessons for singing or playing an instrumemt, I cam tell you right now that it will help you much more than you think it will. Some people think of taking private lessons as extra help for people who are struggling, but most of the time it's people who are already really good doing something extra to get better. Taking private lessons is a great way for students to focus on their individual skills amd make them better than they already are. Having someone who specializes in your instrument and getting some one on one time to talk to them and learn from them is so helpful for students who are finding their way through the wide world of music. You can get the same thing from watching online tutorials and following along, and it'll cost you a lot less most likely, but it won't quite have the same effect. Regardless, both of these methods can be helpful to people who are starting a new instrument or want to get even better at what they already play.

Creating Your Own Sound

This particular outlet is a little more difficult to describe than the previous ones. There are many ways to simply get involved in music, but what about creating your own? This is the most difficult part about making music. Althought it's not as common as it was 20 hears ago, starting a band is a great way to start off a life of music your own unique way. You and the other people in your band can find and create your own sound together in a more collaborative way than anything else. You get to create your own beats, melodies, harmonies, and lyrics with the people you love working with. How much better could music life get?

Songwriting/composing is just the fancy term for creating your own sound. This is literally the most difficult aspect of music. It makes you think about your feelings more than you ever thought you would have to, and sometimes it can get confusing. Once you learn how to actually write music, you have to be consistent in writing it, or the creative flow might be hard to get back into. Taking a class on how to properly write good music that people will actually want to hear isn't a bad idea. There isn't much else for me to explain about this topic, it's one of those things you have to know and feel rather than have it be explained to you. That's all there is too it!
